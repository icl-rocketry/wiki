ICL Rocketry Wiki : Dummy Motor Block

Created by Goncalves Guerra Guimaraes Matias, Joao, last modified on Feb 02, 2020

To analyse the behaviour of the selected motor before the final payload assembly was assembled, a dummy block was needed which had similar moment of inertia.

This block is made of 3D printed PETG and features an attachment point to the motor shaft on the bottom. There are 30 holes on each side wall to fix M6 bolts onto which can be used to vary the desired moment of inertia.

The block is 80 x 80 x 100 mm. The projected mass of the unloaded block is 91.4 g and the moment of inertia around its rotating axis is 1.679 x105 gmm-2 (assuming material density of 1.27 gcm-3).

25 Jan 2020:

  1. Plan to spec the motor by producing characteristic curves (ref mech eng notes).
  2. CAD model for top and bottom "U"s. Try get done by 28 Jan.
  3. Look into H bridge, encoder.

02 Feb 2020:

  • Printed mass of unloaded block is 80.5 g and so its moment of inertia is calculated to be 1.479 x105 gmm-2.
  • 16 M6 bolts (6 g) each with 3 corresponding nuts (approx. 2 g each) were used in the configuration pictured above to raise the overall moment of inertia to approximately 4.927 x105 gmm-2. This is slightly above the projected moment of inertia of the CAD model for the camera setup.
  • Box was successfully mated with motor
