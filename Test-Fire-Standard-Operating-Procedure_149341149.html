ICL Rocketry Wiki : Test-Fire Standard Operating Procedure

Created by Geragidis, Filippos, last modified on Feb 01, 2020

This article details the operating procedure for test-fires from January, 2020 onward. This document is a record of the chain of events that are expected to occur before each test-fire that is to be conducted at Imperial College Silwood Park Campus. Non-conformance to this standard procedure will be considered as a lapse in safety, and follow-up action and consequences may follow in such an event.

  

External Testing at Imperial College Silwood Campus


Administrative Matters

  1. The intention to test must be conveyed to the Propulsion Safety Officer at least 7 days in advance of the desired test commencement day. This request should include the following information, in the form of a mission plan:
    1. Desired test commencement time
    2. Expected number and duration of test-fires
    3. Testing objectives and procedures
    4. Personnel involved
    5. Logistical requirements
    6. Any other pertinent information
  2. A Chief Operator and Electronics Technician and Nitrous Specialist are to be appointed for the test. The previous two roles, as well as the Propulsion Safety Officer must all be members of ICLR leadership, except under circumstance of necessary unavailability.
  3. The Safety Team is to perform a risk assessment of the desired activity. Should the risk assessment not fulfill all safety requirements, the activity is not to proceed.
  4. The Safety Team is also to inform the relevant contacts from the Silwood Park Campus regarding the test(s) that are going to be conducted, with the same level of detail as with (1).


Storage

  1. The Nitrous Oxide tank is to be stored in a gas cage (well-ventilated) near the Hamilton building at Silwood Park Campus. (Provisional), with a protective rain cover.
  2. The engine, test rig, and N20 transport trolley (without tank) are to be stored in the Drive Field laboratory (Provisional).
  3. All consumables, sensors and electronics are to be stored in an airtight box at Imperial College South Kensington Campus, in a locked cupboard (Provisional).

 

Pre-Test-fire Preparations and Engine Assembly

Transport and advance checks:

  1. The weather forecast for the Silwood Campus must be checked at 2000hrs on the day before the departure. Should there be forecasted inclement weather, the test must be postponed.
  2. The weather forecast should be checked again at 1 hour before the departure time. Similarly, should there be inclement weather forecast, the test must be postponed.
  3. All personnel leaving the South Kensington campus for the Silwood Park Campus must be dressed appropriately in long sleeves, long trousers/jeans, and covered shoes. Non-conformance results in denied travel to the test site. There must also be enough PPE to protect absolutely all personnel during the test-fire, as well as relevant members during assembly.
  4. The equipment checklist (appended) is to be completed prior to departure from South Kensington Campus.
  5. All personnel present at the test site must sign into the attendance log.
  6. All personnel are to be briefed regarding the following matters:
    1. Exclusion zone during test-fires;
    2. Location of fire-extinguishing materials, and first-aid kit in the area;
    3. Use of airhorns for warnings and rapid information dissemination;
    4. Evacuation route;
    5. The use of personal protective equipment (ear defenders) during testing.

Before operations begin:

  1. The Chief Operator is to ensure that on the engine assembly table in the laboratory, the following documents are available and clearly visible:
    1. General Test-fire Standard Operating Procedure;
    2. Test-Specific Operation Procedure (for the actual tests being carried out on the day);
    3. Assembly and preparation checklist.
  2. All personnel put on high-vis vests.
  3. The engine is assembled - all active members of personnel equipped with all relevant PPE (detailed in the attached Risk Assessment document).
  4. Immediately before any tests are conducted, personnel is split into three groups:
    1. Operations group (Chief Operator, Electronics Technician, Safety Officer) - behind Drive Field Laboratory;
    2. Left group (half of remaining personnel) -  to the left of Drive Field Inn, stopping oncoming traffic;
    3. Right group (other half of remaining personnel) -  to the right of Drive Field Inn, stopping oncoming traffic.
      1. Note that, due to the above configuration and the requirement for transportation of the nitrous oxide tank, the minimum number of personnel required is 3 (Operations group)+1 (Left)+1 (Right)=5. 
      2. Current assignments (subject to change for each test; confirmation and training completed at least 7 days in advance of activity): Chief Operator: William Harradence; Electronics Technician: Shreeyam Kacker; Safety Officer: Filippos Geragidis; Nitrous Specialist: Rafal Sokolowski
  5. The green light is to be given by the Chief Operator and Electronics Technician, upon completion and checking of the checklist.

Setup Procedure:

  1. Collect parts from Drive Field laboratory.
  2. Inspect all items for transport/storage-induced damage.
  3. Nitrous tank setup:
    1. Transport N2O tank from gas cage to Drive Field Inn, using transport trolley - at least 5 members of personnel required. The tank is fastened to the trolley using ratchet straps, and maintained in an upright position at all times during transport.
    2. The tank inverted stand should be fixed to the top of the tank while in the upright position, and the regulator attached.
    3. The tank should be carefully inverted by laying it flat on the ground, then slowly raising it back up onto the inverted stand. The tank should then be placed back on the trolley in the inverted position, and re-secured. All personnel performing this task must be wearing UK certified safety shoes.
  4. Skip for blowdown test: Insert 2 fuel grains, ensuring that the one facing the nozzle is flush with the end of the combustion chamber. 
  5. Nozzle Assembly:
    1. Fasten nozzle closure to nozzle casing using 2 M3 screws, ensuring that enough washers are used such that the bolts do not reach the end of the threaded holes. 
    2. Insert the nozzle, ensuring the correct orientation (diverging section towards exhaust).
    3. Insert alumina post-combustion chamber lining.
    4. Insert the 2 relevant O-rings into the grooves on the nozzle casing flange.
    5. Attach the nozzle assembly to the combustion chamber using 6 M4 bolts, ensuring that neither the nozzle nor fuel grains fall out in the process.
      1. Nuts must be tightened in a star pattern to ensure even o-ring compression.
  6. Igniter installation:
    1. Insert the 4 o-rings into the injector head.
    2. Insert the appropriate injector plate.
    3. Attach the injector assembly to the combustion chamber, using 6 M4 bolts.
      1. Nuts must be tightened in a star shape to ensure even o-ring compression.
  7. Secure the test bay onto the ground using the four pegs.
  8. Fasten the completed engine onto the test rig.
    1. Position the collars appropriately.
    2. Close the clamps.
    3. Engage the M12 load cell rod.
  9. Prepare electronics - The ignition arming switch must be off during this process, and the power to the master solenoid must be disconnected.
    1. Servo.
    2. DaQ.
    3. Battery box.
    4. Thermocouples.
      1. Install tank thermocouple, and connect to DaQ.
      2. Connect engine thermocouples to DaQ
    5. Load cell
      1. Connect load cell lead to DaQ.
    6. Ensure correct servo wiring.
    7. Ensure all connections are firm.
    8. Ensure there are batteries installed.
    9. Ensure the SD card is installed.
    10. Connect signal line.
    11. Test valve actuation: Repeat if fails.
    12. Position electronics box ensuring no tangled or caught leads and close lid.
  10. Set up cameras, ensuring that they are in focus and recording.
  11. All non-essential personnel should now retreat to outside the exclusion zone, with 2 designated personnel (one of which is the Nitrous Specialist) to remain to perform the final setup steps.
  12. Connect oxidizer line, wearing face shield, boiler suit, and insulating gloves.
  13. Slowly open the cylinder valve to full-open.
  14. Slowly open the regulator valve to full-open, as to prevent pressure shock.
  15. The final 2 personnel should retreat outside the exclusion zone.
  16. Ensure no other activity is taking place in the exclusion zone.

Test-Fire

  1. The Chief Operator, Electronics Technician and Propulsion Safety Officer to be at the command location at all times.
  2. For rapid communication with all personnel in the testing area, the airhorn patterns outlined in the emergency procedures should be followed.
  3. Traffic is to be stopped going into the exclusion, using clear signage carried by personnel in the Left and Right groups.
  4. A 'cold-fire'/blowdown test is conducted to ensure there are no leaks in the system.
  5. Upon insertion of the fuel grains, reassembly of the engine, and clearing of the exclusion zone, hot-fire tests may be conducted by the Chief Operator using the remote control station.

Tear-down Procedure:

  1. Disarm the ignition system and disconnect the power to the master solenoid.
  2. Ensure exclusion zone is safe to enter - note that personnel are only allowed in upon disarming.
  3. Only 2 specified personnel may enter the exclusion zone to shut off the nitrous supply. They should be wearing facemasks and gloves. They will shut the nitrous regulator, then retreat outside the exclusion zone.
  4. The command center should be signaled to vent remaining nitrous between the regulator and master valve. Power to the master valve should then be disconnected.
  5. Other personnel may now enter the exclusion zone.
  6. Check engine temperature with IR thermometer to ensure it is safe to handle.
  7. Disconnect oxidizer line, wearing face shield, boiler suit, and insulating gloves.
  8. Stop camera recording.
  9. Detach tank thermocouple.
  10. Inspect engine, record observations and take pictures.
  11. Disassemble test rig:
    1. Disengage M12 load cell rod.
    2. Open clamps and remove engine.
  12. With gloves, remove injector assembly:
    1. Replace any that show signs of damage.
    2. Must replace innermost flange o-ring.
    1. Perform visual inspection & collect photos.
    2. Inspect o-rings:
  13. With gloves, remove nozzle assembly, ensuring nozzle does not fall out. 
    1. Inspect o-rings (see 7.b).
    2. Inspect nozzle, then remove and place in safe location for further inspection (ablation).
    3. Clean nozzle casing internals and install new nozzle.
  14. With gloves, inspect residual fuel grain.
  15. Remove residual fuel grain, while attempting to keep grain intact.
  16. Use scraper to clean inside of combustion chamber.
  17. Perform initial set-up procedure from step 4.

Post-Test-fire

  1. The engine and test rig are to be stored safely in the laboratory.
  2. A safety debrief is to be conducted where necessary.
  3. Nitrous tank teardown:
    1. Ensure power to master solenoid is disconnected
    2. While wearing appropriate PPE, Nitrous Specialist closes regulator valve, then closes cylinder valve.
