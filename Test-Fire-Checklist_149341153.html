ICL Rocketry Wiki : Test-Fire Checklist

Created by Geragidis, Filippos, last modified on Feb 01, 2020

This page contains a working draft of the pre-test-fire checklist which must be completed prior to any tests conducted at Silwood Park campus or otherwise.


Administrative

  • Has a successful risk assessment been conducted for this activity?
  • Is everyone attending accounted for on the attendance sheet?
  • Have the relevant personnel and nearby residents at Silwood Park Campus been contacted prior to this activity?

 

Personnel

  • Is the attendance sheet free of persons not essential to the entire activity?
  • Is all active personnel appropriately equipped with and wearing PPE for the activity?
  • Is all personnel assembling the engine trained?
  • Is the operator of the electronics trained?
  • Is all personnel fully familiar with the standard testing procedure?
  • Has all non-essential personnel (to the test-fire) been removed from the immediate premises during tests?

 

Environment

  • Has the area surrounding the engine been cleared of flammable materials?
  • Has the weather been checked for extreme conditions 1 hour prior to the departure?
  • Are the locations of the fire extinguisher, first-aid kit, and other fire-extinguishing materials known to all personnel participating in the test?
  • Is the evacuation route known to all personnel?

 

Hardware

  • Has the hardware been checked for damage and/or tampering due to transport?
  • Has the testbed been attached rigidly to the ground?
  • Has the engine been fastened together appropriately?
  • Have all main controls been connected to the liquid fuel tank and engine?
  • Has the fluid feed system been tested via cold flow run(s)?

 

Electronics

  • Has the control computer been connected and the sensors checked?

 

Others

  • Have all other potential hazards (unique to the test case) been accounted for?

 

If the response to any of the above questions is NO, the activity MUST NOT proceed until all safety checks are carried out as above.
