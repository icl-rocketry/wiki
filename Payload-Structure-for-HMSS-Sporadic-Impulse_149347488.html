Quoting Sa Cup's rules:  "Payload is defined as being replaceable with a ballast of the same mass, with no change to the launch vehicle’strajectory in reaching the target apogee, or its’ successful recovery"

We will have a Technology Demonstration Payload (3U) that will consist of mechanically compensating for the rocket's rotation to allow for a stable 360-degree recording of the flight (currently -> rotating plate on static shaft with cameras, plate controlled by motor and gears). This technology as other applications such as blah blah blah ... (etc)

Shall be suitable to also enter SDL payload challenge.


Design constraints:

  • minimum payload mass 8.8lbs (~4kg) - can have up to 2.25 lbs of "boilerplate mass"
  • bonus points are awarded for following CubeSat standard
  • needs to be able to be weighed independently of launch vehicle
  • can't contain significant quantities of lead or hazardous materials
  • *ideally, COM in all 3 axes is "close" to the geometric center*
  • *rail with >8.5mm*
  • vehicle must have COTS barometric pressure altimeter with on-board data storage
  • shall carry a radio beacon or similar transmitter to aid locatting




Note: any constraint with a "*" is not Sa Cup requirement but rather a design preference following "CubeSat Desing Specification" by CalPoly (used by Nasa's CSLI). ← extra bueno if we can also follow these
